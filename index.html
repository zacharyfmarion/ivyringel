About Ivy

Bassoonist Ivy Ringel recently relocated to Houston, TX from Rochester, NY to begin her Master’s degree at Rice University, where she is studying with Benjamin Kamins. In 2014, Ivy won the grand prize at the Meg Quigley Vivaldi Competition, an international solo competition for young female bassoonists. Ivy is also a 2016 winner of the Yamaha Young Performing Artist competition and the Eastman School of Music’s 2015 concerto competition, where she performed the Hummel Concerto in F Major with the Eastman Philharmonia. Ivy’s orchestral work includes performances with the Rochester Philharmonic Orchestra, Buffalo Philharmonic Orchestra and Symphoria.

Ivy’s chamber music interests led her to tour Europe with the Eastman Wind Ensemble’s Harmonie this summer. In previous summers, she has attended the Round Top Music Festival, Chautauqua Music Festival, and the Governor’s School of North Carolina. She also is a founding member of The Eclectet, a woodwind quintet formed in 2012. The Eclectet is committed to educational outreach, performing in the Museum of Play, the George Eastman House and other Rochester landmarks. Contemporary music is another passion of Ivy’s, frequently performing with Eastman’s Musica Nova, conducted by Brad Lubman, and Ossia, Eastman’s student-run contemporary music ensemble.

Ivy began her musical life at age 5 as a violinist. She later switched to the bassoon, beginning her bassoon study at age 12 in her hometown of Hillsborough, North Carolina. Ivy has had further studies with Kristin Wolfe Jensen, Michael Burns, and Jeff Robinson. This spring, Ivy graduated from the Eastman School of Music, where she studied with John Hunt and Charlie Bailey. Her other interests include cooking and attending live music shows of all genres.
